What Is Contract Staffing

A transitory team member on a third-party payroll

Contract staffing is a transitory member of staff working with your organisation while sitting on a third-party payroll. It is an accepted practice in multinational companies globally, and many large Indian entities now staff entire functions or categories of personnel through manpower staffing partners.

As organisations focus on their core expertise to stay competitive, non-core functions are outsourced. The personnel for these functions are sourced and managed by the staffing partner. Service Level Agreements typically run from three to eleven months, with no friction around hiring, retaining, or releasing manpower.

Contract staffing is a co-employment association between three parties:

  • 01

    The staffing partner and the client corporation sign a service contract; the personnel and the staffing partner already have a service association.

  • 02

    The personnel performs the work at the client's end, while remaining on the staffing partner's payroll for compensation, benefits, and statutory compliance.

Co-employment Model

Three parties. Clear roles.

No grey zones. Each party knows exactly what they own, so the engagement runs cleanly from day one to release.

Client Corporation

Defines the role, supervises day-to-day work, sets the deliverables, and provides the workplace. No payroll administration on your side.

Staffing Partner

Sources, screens, and onboards the personnel. Owns payroll, statutory compliance, benefits, replacements, and the SLA.

Personnel

Holds an employment contract with the staffing partner, performs work at the client's end, and is paid through the staffing partner.
